Advancing LMS Security & System Flexibility

JB
James Benedict March 24, 2026
Week 5
Week 5 marked a major milestone as our project reached 50% completion. My focus during this period was enhancing the security and flexibility of the Learning Management System (LMS). We successfully integrated a "3-chance pause" feature for assessment exams. This system is designed to monitor student activity; if a student leaves the page or attempts to pause more than three times, the system automatically triggers a submission to maintain academic integrity and prevent cheating.
We also finalized the dynamic Explore Page. By developing a backend structure that allows administrators to relayout and manage content in real-time, we’ve empowered them to update the system without requiring further code changes. However, development wasn't without its challenges. We dealt with navigation inconsistencies, where the "back" button incorrectly redirected users. I resolved this by utilizing JavaScript session storage to track and preserve the state of dynamic content, ensuring a seamless flow.
Additionally, we faced recurring XAMPP MySQL service crashes that interrupted our development. By troubleshooting port conflicts and optimizing our database configuration, we restored environment stability. This experience was a great lesson in local server management and the vital role that system logs play in diagnosing professional development hurdles. As we head into the next phase, I am excited to work on the automated Certification System.
