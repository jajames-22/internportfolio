Administrative Integration & Final Review

JB
James Benedict April 14, 2026
Week 8
During Week 8, we focused heavily on organizational restructuring and refining the user experience of the Learning Management System (LMS). A key highlight was the successful introduction of a new administrative role for the Curriculum Implementation Division (CID). This update decentralizes our content management workflow by allowing specialized staff to independently handle module creation, material uploading, and assessment evaluation, which significantly improves the operational efficiency of the division.
In addition to the restructuring, we focused on hardening system security. We identified and successfully patched authorization issues specifically associated with the CID role, ensuring that access levels are strictly enforced and that sensitive administrative tools remain protected. I also finalized our custom email layout templates. This ensures that every system-generated notification maintains a cohesive, professional branding consistent with official Department of Education standards.
As we stand at an 80% completion milestone, our administrative hierarchy is finally stable. Our primary focus is now shifting toward the formal presentation to the Information Technology Officer (ITO) and the Schools Division Superintendent (SDS). We aim to demonstrate the full capabilities of the LMS to leadership, ensuring we incorporate their final feedback as we enter the polishing phase of the project.
